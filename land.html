Land Turtles and Tortoises

This page will talk all about land turtles and tortoises.
These are turles who spend their time primarily on land.
Although many are semi aquatic, they aren't sea creatures.
Come learn about some of the exciting different types of land turtles!

Box Turtles

There are 4 different species of box turtles. The common box turtle, the coahuilan box turtle the spotted box turtle, and the ornate box turtle. Among these there are several subspecies. They have a dommed shell that makes them look box like. This is where it gets its name. Box turtles can swim, but they are not very good at it. They are omnivoric and eat a mix of plants, bugs and eggs.

The box turtle is the start reptile in 4 US states:
Kansas, Missouri, North Carolina and Tennessee.

Tortoises

There are 49 different species of tortoises. The Galapagos tortoise is the largest tortoise and also the longest living, reaching over 150 years old! All tortoises are primarily land dwelling although some are partly aquatic. Most species of tortoises don't lay more than 20 eggs. Most species of tortoises are herbavoric. They eat grass, flowers, fruit and various other plants.

The Tortoise and the Hare was an Aesop's fable
in which the tortoise beat the hare in a race.

Pond Sliders

The pond slider is a species of turtle native to the southwest United States of America and Mexico. There are 3 subspecies of pond sliders: Yellow-Bellied Slider, Red-Eared Slider, and Cumberland slider. These turtles are often seen as an evasive species. They split their time swimming in the water and walking on land. They often bask in the sun near birds nests in order to eat their eggs. They have an omnivoric diet.

The Red-Eared Slider pond slider
is the most popular pet turtle in the US.

Snake Necked Turtles

There are several species of snaked necked turtles. They are called this because of there long necks. They are native to Australia and its surrounding islands. Unlike other species of turtles, the snake neck turtles don't pull their necks into their shells. Rather they tuck their heads around the side on their bodies. They mostly eat fish and are great swimmers. They are semi-aquatic and spend their time on land and in water.

The snake neck turtles can spray a stinky spray when stessed
similar to skunks.
